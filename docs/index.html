The Ancient Scroll

In 2500 BC, Sumerian kings strapped clay tablets to pigeons and sent them across Mesopotamia. In 1150, the Sultan of Baghdad built towers of carrier pigeons to relay intelligence across the Islamic empire. In 1870, during the Siege of Paris, pigeons carried microfilm dispatches over Prussian lines — 150,000 messages on the wings of a single flock.

The dove has always been the messenger. The scroll has always been the medium. The ink has always been the truth.

The Sacred Symbol

In Genesis, the dove returned to Noah carrying an olive branch — proof that the flood had passed, that new life was possible. In art, in faith, in culture, the white dove has always meant the same thing: peace after chaos, clarity after confusion, truth after silence.

In an age of information overload, we believe health & technology journalism needs a messenger that represents purity of signal over noise. The dove doesn't shout. It arrives.
